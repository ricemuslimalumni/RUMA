Vision

The Muslim community has been beckoning for a centralized network that is academic and career-centered to connect Muslims doing amazing things. We want to provide that network, and envision an initiative led for students, by students.

This project was started by the MIT MSA, and has since spread to several universities across the USA. The Texas branch is currently being administered through the Rice University Muslim Alumni group. Our goal is to foster a large-scale network that is built on meritocracy and being Muslim, rather than culture and nationality.

The idea is simple. We have gone through the process. We have faced the challenges high school students are currently experiencing. We want to offer you the assistance which we needed when we were in high school. Whether it’s answering questions about the application process, proofreading essays, conducting mock interviews, or connecting you with internships, we want to help you out in any way that we can.

A Muslim should leave his mark wherever he goes. Teachers and professors should be impressed by their Muslim students. From UIL to Science Bowl, Muslim competitors should leave their peers awestruck. A Muslim's work ethic and commitment to excellence must be unparalleled. We want to help you on this journey to accomplish your dreams and change the world.

"Acquire knowledge and impart it to the people." - Al-Tirmidhi
